Why caffeine timing matters

Caffeine works by blocking adenosine, the molecule that builds up through the day to make you feel sleepy. So caffeine doesn’t add energy — it masks your sleep pressure. Even when you manage to fall asleep with caffeine in your system, studies show it reduces deep sleep and overall sleep quality, so you wake less rested without knowing why.

Because the half-life is roughly 5–6 hours, a strong coffee at 4 PM can still have a meaningful dose circulating at midnight. As a rule of thumb, stopping caffeine at least 6 hours before bed lets most of it clear. Sensitivity varies a lot with genetics, age, and tolerance, so if you’re a slow metaboliser you may need an even earlier cut-off.
